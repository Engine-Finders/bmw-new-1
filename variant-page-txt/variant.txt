It depends entirely on which of the two engine generations your Defender has. The 12J (1983–1986) is widely regarded as a simple, robust, if agricultural, indirect-injection diesel. On the 19J turbo diesel, cylinder head cracking and head gasket failure are the headline issues. Symptoms: Cold-start rattle, metallic ticking from engine, engine warning light, loss of power, rough running.
Typical Mileage: 60,000–120,000 miles — problems can appear much earlier .
Repair Cost: £1,500–£3,000 (chain replacement, guides, tensioners).
Replacement Cost: N/A — repair only if caught early; catastrophic failure requires full engine replacement .
Urgency: 🔴 Immediate — at first sign of rattle, book inspection today.
Our recommendation: The 204DTD's timing chain system is a known weak point. The components that make up the timing system are prone to failure because of the weak materials used . Plastic guides can break and drop debris into the oil pickup, causing spun bearings . If the chain stretches too far, it can "jump" a tooth or snap, causing pistons to strike open valves in this interference engine . Regular oil changes (every 8,000 miles, not the suggested 24,000) are critical .

Symptoms: Loss of power, excessive smoke, whistling noise from engine bay, engine management light.
Typical Mileage: 60,000–120,000 miles.
Repair Cost: £800–£2,000 (turbocharger replacement).
Replacement Cost: N/A — repair only; turbo failure can cause catastrophic engine damage.
Urgency: 🔴 Immediate — book inspection urgently if symptoms appear.
Our recommendation: The turbocharger on all diesel Ingenium engines is composed of components and materials that are seemingly not sufficient for its purpose . A failed turbocharger can cause engine failure due to the intake of oil and possible debris . When excessive amounts of oil pass through the intake, the engine hydro-locks, bending conrods . If you notice any whistling, smoke, or power loss, have the turbo inspected immediately.
