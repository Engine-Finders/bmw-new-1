FAQ
How much does a Jaguar AJ-V8 engine replacement cost?
A reconditioned AJ-V8 typically costs £4,500–£7,500 supply only, varying by displacement (4.0L vs 4.2L) and whether it's naturally aspirated or supercharged. Fitting adds £650–£1,300 depending on labour rates.
Is the Jaguar AJ-V8 a reliable engine?
The AJ-V8 earns 3.5★ out of 5, based on UK enquiry data and specialist reports — a smooth, characterful V8 with genuine longevity potential, provided the timing chain tensioners have been updated.
What is the most common Jaguar AJ-V8 failure?
Plastic timing chain tensioners on pre-2002 4.0L engines — they crack, allowing chain slack and catastrophic valve/piston contact. The 4.2L received revised, more durable tensioners.
Which Jaguar models use the AJ-V8?
The AJ-V8 powered the XK8/XKR (X100), XJ8/XJR (X308/X350), S-Type/S-Type R, and early XF (X250) — eight Jaguar models in total across naturally aspirated and supercharged forms.
Is the Jaguar AJ-V8 the same as Land Rover's version?
Yes — the same AJ-V8 family was also fitted to the Land Rover Range Rover L322 (4.4L variant). The core architecture is identical, though Land Rover applications use different ECU calibrations and ancillaries.

Is the Jaguar XK straight-six a reliable engine?

What is the most common Jaguar XK straight-six failure?
