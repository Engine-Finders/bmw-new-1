SECTION 6 — FAQ
1. Cost — How much does a 200Tdi or 300Tdi engine replacement cost?
A reconditioned 200Tdi or 300Tdi typically costs £3,000–£5,500 supply only, with used units from £1,000–£2,000. Fitting adds £900–£2,500 depending on labour rates and vehicle access.
2. Reliability — Is the 200Tdi / 300Tdi a reliable engine?
Yes — both are widely considered among the most reliable Land Rover engines ever built. Their fully mechanical fuel systems, lack of complex electronics, and simple OHV design make them exceptionally durable, with many exceeding 250,000–300,000 miles.
3. Common failure — What is the most common 200Tdi / 300Tdi failure?
Head gasket failure from overheating is the most common serious issue — typically caused by cooling system neglect. Early 300Tdi units also suffered timing belt idler alignment problems, and 200Tdi crank dampers can disintegrate.
4. Fitment — Which Land Rover models use the 200Tdi / 300Tdi?
The 200Tdi and 300Tdi powered three core Land Rover models: the Defender (90/110), Discovery Series 1, and Range Rover Classic.
5. Longevity — How long does the 200Tdi / 300Tdi last?
With proper maintenance, 200Tdi and 300Tdi engines routinely exceed 250,000 miles, with many well-maintained examples reaching 300,000+ miles. Regular timing belt changes every 80,000 km (~50,000 miles) and cooling system maintenance are key to achieving this lifespan.

The AJ20D6 is a modern and capable straight‑six diesel (4.0★) with strong real‑world feedback. Early examples (2020–2021) have reported AdBlue system and EGR cooler issues, but JLR has addressed these with software updates and hardware revisions.
3. Common failure — What is the most common AJ20D6 failure?
AdBlue system issues (NOx sensors, heaters, and crystallisation) are the most common reported problems, particularly on early 2020–2021 vehicles. EGR cooler leaks and occasional DPF clogging from urban use are also reported. No major bottom‑end issues are known.
4. Fitment — Which Land Rover models use the AJ20D6?
The AJ20D6 powers the latest‑generation Range Rover (L460), Range Rover Sport (L461), and Defender (L663) — spanning D250, D300, and D350 outputs.
5. Shared — Is the Land Rover AJ20D6 the same as Jaguar's version?
Yes — the AJ20D6 is shared with Jaguar fitments in the F‑Pace, XF, and XJ. The engine is mechanically identical, though ECU calibrations and ancillaries differ by model.

1. Cost — How much does an SI6 (B6324S) engine replacement cost?
A reconditioned SI6 3.2 typically costs £2,200–£3,500 supply only, with used units from £800–£1,600. Fitting adds £800–£1,500 depending on labour rates and vehicle access.
2. Reliability — Is the Volvo SI6 (B6324S) a reliable engine?
Yes — the SI6 is considered a reliable and refined petrol engine (4.0★). Its smooth inline‑six design offers excellent power delivery and durability. However, early examples (2006–2009) have reported timing chain tensioner issues, so service history is critical.
3. Common failure — What is the most common SI6 failure?
Timing chain tensioner failure is the most critical — particularly on early engines (2006–2009) where the tensioner design was prone to failure, causing chain stretch and catastrophic valve/piston interference. Oil consumption and oil leaks from the timing cover are also reported.
4. Fitment — Which Land Rover models use the SI6?
In Land Rover application, the SI6 (B6324S) powered the Freelander 2 (L359) from 2006 to 2014. The engine was also used across Volvo's XC60, XC90, S80, and V70 models.
5. Longevity — How long does the SI6 (B6324S) last?
With proper maintenance and early timing chain tensioner replacement where needed, SI6 engines routinely exceed 150,000–200,000 miles. Regular oil changes and chain inspections are key to achieving this lifespan, particularly on pre‑2010 examples.
